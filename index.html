Jeremy Zhengqi Huang

My research lies in Human-Computer Interaction, with applications in accessibility. Specifically, my research focuses on developing and studying tools that enables accessible retrieval and creations of information in physical and digital worlds.

I am part of the Accessibility Lab advised by Dhruv Jain. I recently graduated with a Master's Degree in Information (UX Research and Design specialization) at the University of Michigan. My undergraduate studies at Ohio State focused on data science and psychology. I also worked as a research assistant at the CAIDe Lab advised by Brittany Shoots-Reinhard.
